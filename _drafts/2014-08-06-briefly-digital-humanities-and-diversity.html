Briefly: Digital Humanities and Diversity

Some resources on digital humanities and inclusivity of what we study, what we learn, how we create/preserve/interpret the past, and who's welcome to participate/read/teach/interpret.

Conferences, behavior. We solve this by not perpetuating it. In the DH world, people can refuse to take part in panels of more than three people that are all male, or try reading only books by women.

Stanford University Libraries has encourages its staff to perpetuate inclusive conferences by participating "only in those conferences that have clear and public anti-harassment policies and/or codes of conduct", or advocating to conference organizers for inclusion of the same.

Readings. Debates in DH has a number of good pieces on digital humanities and diversity (of projects, participants, audiences):

  • "Why Are the Digital Humanities So White? or Thinking the Histories of Race and Computation" by Tara McPherson
  • "Disability, Universal Design, and the Digital Humanities" by George Williams
  • "The Digital Humanities and Its Users" by Charlie Edwards
  • "What Do Girls Dig?" by Bethany Nowviskie
  • "Canons, Close Reading, and the Evolution of Method" by Matthew Wilkens, on how the digital humanities can deal with the negative effects of literary canon

"Can Information Be Unfettered? Race and the New Digital Humanities Canon" by Amy Earhart
