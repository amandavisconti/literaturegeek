Intro (contextualizing engagement, not scholarly edition)

Success:

Other than my favorite favicon ever:...what features of the site do I still like?

  1. The part of the site I developed for an independent study in the UM Department of Education was pedagogically biased ** original specialization: online multimedia education
  2. layout and attention/distraction; context right when you need it; big help and contact buttons
  3. toggling/customizing help: u2: all, none, bio, hist, vocab, style, puzzles
  4. episode time (macro view of text was idea)
  5. aimed at first-time readers (small blurb on why you would want to read Ulysses and a longer one on what's so hard about reading Ulysses)

Failure:

  1. Ridiculously nested tags
  2. Non-intuitive navigation (hard to find text pages, but did include site use tutorial page: http://www.ulyssesulysses.com/howtouse.shtml)
  3. Image source citations ("For image references, view page source and look under the alt attribute.", but pulls images to separate page feed was good)
  4. Color palette
  5. No bookmarking
